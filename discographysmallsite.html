DISCOGRAPHY

Get Stoked On It!(2007)

Get Stoked on It! is the debut studio album by American rock band The Wonder Years, released through No Sleep Records on October 30, 2007. The album features guest vocals from Rachel Minton (Zolof the Rock and Roll Destroyer), Bob Wilson (Letxdown), and Brooke Schwartz (CDC). The album was remixed, remastered and given a new cover on May 15, 2012. Dan Campbell described the original as a "train wreck". He said about the album, "If you like the record, enjoy the new mixes. If you hate the record, I'm on your side."

The Upsides (2010)

The Upsides is a part of a trilogy (along with Suburbia I've Given You All and Now I'm Nothing and The Greatest Generation) that dealt with vocalist Dan Campbell's struggles of being scared, loneliness and feeling lost. It's is about fighting back. Although it seemed the members of the band had been beaten into misery in their early 20s, after a month of writing with the band living in his home, they emerged with a newly adopted 'we're not sad anymore' philosophy.

Suburbia I've Given You All and Now I'm Nothing (2011)

The title of the album and some of its lyrics reference the Allen Ginsberg poem "America" which according to the albums liner notes served as the primary inspiration for the record. The track "Came Out Swinging" begins with a sample of Ginsberg reading the poem. This album is a part of a trilogy (along with The Upsides and The Greatest Generation) that dealt with being scared, loneliness and feeling lost.

The Greatest Generation (2013)

This album was about the end of the war lead singer Dan Campbell had within himself fighting depression and anxiety. The title is taken from the term coined by Tom Brokaw about how the generation that fought in World War II was 'the greatest generation'. The Greatest Generation is the final part of a trilogy (along with The Upsides and Suburbia I've Given You All and Now I'm Nothing) that dealt with being scared, loneliness and feeling lost.

No Closer to Heaven (2015)

No Closer to Heaven is the fifth studio album by American rock band The Wonder Years. Struggling with writer's block, vocalist Daniel Campbell spoke with several friends who were in bands for inspiration. No Closer to Heaven is a concept album, detailing the loss of a loved one. The album was recorded between March and April 2015. It was produced by Steve Evetts at The Omen Room.

Sister Cities (2018)

After the release of No Closer to Heaven in 2015, The Wonder Years spent two years on a massive world tour to support the album. Along the way, Dan Campbell kept a journal to document experiences and feelings, and the band took photographs of everything they saw. Following the tour, they went through the collections and highlighted excerpts they felt were meaningful. Lyrics began to take shape, and the band worked to compose music that fit the mood of the lyrical content. Sister Cities came together from this process as "a record that seeks to create unity in a world that is becoming increasingly segregated".
